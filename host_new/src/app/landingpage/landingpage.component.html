Impacted Lives

Helped at the right time

Relief for Sukbogi's family

Making most of the opportunity

A new beginning

My wife and I are both disabled. We have two infant daughters too. I am a tailor but do not have any work now. World Vision India came at the right time and gave us the food kit. We have no words to thank World Vision India.

This support came at the right time. We struggled a lot to feed our children. Now we can buy 20 kg of rice and other grocery items, says Sukbogi. Both Sukbogi and her husband were daily wage labourers had no work for more than three months.

When the lives of women change, their families change and ultimately their children are benefitted. We thank World Vision India for making this possible in our community, says Sudha. One sewing machine has changed Sudha's life. In addition to supporting her family, she takes sewing classes for women in her community too.

Now I earn a decent income and need not beg others for my wage for the day, says Sujata. When Sujata's husband died in 2015 she struggled to make ends meet. With the help of the pushcart she is able to sell chat items and earn anywhere between Rs. 200 to Rs. 700 a day.
